Victim Compensation Programs

Available Programs

Criminal Injuries Compensation Board (CICB)

Eligibility:

  • Injured as a result of a violent crime, or family members of deceased victims.
  • Injuries can be physical or psychological.
  • Crime must have occurred in Ontario.
  • Police report must have been made (charges and convictions not necessary).
  • Must apply within two years of the incident date (extensions possible).

Documents Needed:

  • Application Form
  • Police Report
  • Medical Records
  • Proof of Expenses
  • Proof of Relationship

Application Process:

  • Contact CICB: 416-326-2900 or 1-800-372-7463, info.cicb@ontario.ca
  • Complete and Submit Application
  • Review and Decision

Example Case:

Jane Doe's Application

Part 1: Applicant Information

  • Name: Jane Doe
  • Address: 123 Main Street, Toronto, ON, M1A 2B3
  • Phone Number: (416) 555-1234
  • Email Address: jane.doe@example.com
  • Date of Birth: January 1, 1980

Part 2: Incident Information

  • Date of Incident: March 15, 2023
  • Location of Incident: 456 Elm Street, Toronto, ON
  • Police Report Number: 2023-123456
  • Description of Incident: Jane was assaulted by an unknown assailant while walking home. She sustained physical injuries and required medical treatment.

Part 3: Injury Information

  • Type of Injury: Physical injuries (bruises, cuts) and psychological trauma (anxiety, PTSD)
  • Medical Treatment Received: Emergency room visit, follow-up appointments with a physician, and counseling sessions.

Part 4: Expenses Incurred

  • Medical Expenses: $1,500 (emergency room visit, follow-up appointments)
  • Counseling Expenses: $800 (10 sessions at $80 each)
  • Other Expenses: $200 (transportation to medical appointments)

Part 5: Supporting Documents

  • Police Report: Attached
  • Medical Records: Attached
  • Receipts for Expenses: Attached

Part 6: Declaration and Signature

  • Declaration: I declare that the information provided in this application is true and accurate to the best of my knowledge.
  • Signature: Jane Doe
  • Date: April 1, 2023

Submitting the Application:

  • Gather Documents: Ensure all supporting documents (police report, medical records, receipts) are attached.
  • Submit Application: Send the completed application form and supporting documents to the CICB office:
    • Mail: Criminal Injuries Compensation Board, 15 Grosvenor Street, Ground Floor, Toronto, ON M7A 2G6
    • Email: info.cicb@ontario.ca

Follow-Up:

  • Contact Information: For any questions or to check the status of the application, contact the CICB at 416-326-2900 or toll-free at 1-800-372-7463.

Financial Assistance for Families of Homicide Victims (FAFHV) Program

Eligibility:

  • Homicide occurred on or after January 1, 2016.
  • Applicant was the victimâ€™s parent or spouse at the time of death.
  • Homicide occurred in Ontario, and applicant resides in Ontario.

Documents Needed:

  • Application Form
  • Death Certificate
  • Proof of Relationship
  • Proof of Residency
  • Police Report

Application Process:

  • Contact FAFHV: 1-855-467-4344 or 416-212-9164
  • Complete and Submit Application
  • Review and Decision within 90 days

Example Case:

John Doe's Application

Part 1: Applicant Information

  • Name: John Doe
  • Address: 789 Maple Avenue, Ottawa, ON, K1A 0B1
  • Phone Number: (613) 555-6789
  • Email Address: john.doe@example.com
  • Date of Birth: February 15, 1975

Part 2: Victim Information

  • Victim's Name: Jane Doe
  • Relationship to Victim: Spouse
  • Date of Birth: March 10, 1980
  • Date of Death: January 20, 2025
  • Location of Homicide: 123 Elm Street, Ottawa, ON

Part 3: Incident Information

  • Police Report Number: 2025-987654
  • Description of Incident: Jane Doe was a victim of homicide during a home invasion. The assailant was unknown, and the case is under investigation.

Part 4: Eligibility Criteria

  • Homicide Date: January 20, 2025 (after January 1, 2016)
  • Residency: John Doe resides in Ontario.
  • Victim's Contribution: Jane Doe did not contribute to her death through criminal activity.
  • Applicant's Criminal Record: John Doe has no convictions or pending charges related to the homicide.

Part 5: Expenses Incurred

  • Funeral Expenses: $7,000 (funeral service, burial costs)
  • Counseling Expenses: $1,200 (15 sessions at $80 each)
  • Other Expenses: $500 (transportation to counseling sessions)

Part 6: Supporting Documents

  • Death Certificate: Attached
  • Proof of Relationship: Marriage certificate attached
  • Proof of Residency: Utility bill attached
  • Police Report: Attached
  • Receipts for Expenses: Attached

Part 7: Declaration and Signature

  • Declaration: I declare that the information provided in this application is true and accurate to the best of my knowledge.
  • Signature: John Doe
  • Date: February 28, 2025

Submitting the Application:

  • Gather Documents: Ensure all supporting documents (death certificate, marriage certificate, proof of residency, police report, receipts) are attached.
  • Submit Application: Send the completed application form and supporting documents to the FAFHV office:
    • Mail: Financial Assistance for Families of Homicide Victims Program, Ministry of the Attorney General, 720 Bay Street, Toronto, ON M7A 2S9
    • Email: fafhv@ontario.ca

Follow-Up:

  • Contact Information: For any questions or to check the status of the application, contact the FAFHV Program at 1-855-467-4344 or 416-212-9164.

Victim Quick Response Program (VQRP)

Eligibility:

  • Victim of violent crime (homicide, attempted murder, serious assault, etc.).
  • Applications for emergency expenses must be within 45 days, counseling within 90 days.
  • Crime occurred in Ontario, funds used in Ontario.

Documents Needed:

  • Application Form
  • Police Report
  • Proof of Expenses
  • Proof of No Other Financial Resources

Application Process:

  • Contact VQRP: 1-888-579-2888
  • Referral through police or victim support agency
  • Work with a caseworker to complete and submit the application
  • Review and Decision

Example Case:

Part 3: Type of Assistance Requested

  • Transportation Expenses: $100 (transportation to medical appointments)

Part 4: Supporting Documents

  • Medical Records: Attached

Restitution for Assault and Battery Victims

Eligibility:

  • Courts can order offenders to pay restitution to victims for monetary losses resulting from bodily or psychological harm or damage to property caused by the crime.

Documents Needed:

  • Proof of Financial Losses: Documentation of monetary losses resulting from bodily or psychological harm or damage to property.
  • Court Documents: Any court documents related to the case.

Application Process:

  • Contact the Court: Speak with the victim services office at the court handling the case to understand the process for requesting restitution.
  • Provide Documentation: Submit documentation of your financial losses to the court.
  • Attend Court Hearings: Attend court hearings where restitution will be discussed and decided.

Example Case:

Mark Johnson's Application

Part 1: Victim Information

  • Name: Mark Johnson
  • Address: 123 Maple Street, Toronto, ON, M4B 1B3
  • Phone Number: (416) 555-1234
  • Email Address: mark.johnson@example.com
  • Date of Birth: June 15, 1985

Part 2: Offender Information

  • Name of Offender: John Doe
  • Court File Number: 2025-987654
  • Court Location: Toronto, ON

Part 3: Financial Losses

  • Medical Expenses: $2,000 (emergency room visit, follow-up appointments)
  • Counseling Expenses: $1,200 (15 sessions at $80 each)
  • Lost Wages: $3,000 (missed work due to injuries)
  • Other Expenses: $500 (transportation to medical appointments)

Part 4: Supporting Documents

  • Medical Records: Attached
  • Receipts for Expenses: Attached
  • Proof of Lost Wages: Pay stubs and employer letter attached

Part 5: Declaration and Signature

  • Declaration: I declare that the information provided in this application is true and accurate to the best of my knowledge.
  • Signature: Mark Johnson
  • Date: March 1, 2025

Submitting the Application:

  • Gather Documents: Ensure all supporting documents (medical records, receipts, proof of lost wages) are attached.
  • Submit Application: Send the completed application form and supporting documents to the court handling the case:
    • Mail: Address provided by the court handling the case.
    • In-Person: Submit directly to the court office.

Follow-Up:

  • Contact Information: For any questions or to check the status of the application, contact the victim services office at the court handling the case.

For more information, visit Ontario.ca or contact local victim support services.
